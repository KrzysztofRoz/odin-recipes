Mochi

Description:

Mochi is a bun-shaped Japanese dessert made from sweet glutinous rice flour, or mochigome. It has a soft, chewy texture that is somewhat elastic. Mochi si traditional dessert in New Years Eve in Japan, but also on Children's Day or Girl's Day. Mochi without filling also can be serve with roast or grill meat and as an addition to the soup.

Time and servings:

  • Prep: 30 mins
  • Cook: 5 mins
  • Additional: 1 hr
  • Total: 1 hr 35 mins
  • Servings: 8
  • Yield: 8 mochi

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup sweetened red bean paste
  • 1 cup glutinous rice flour
  • 1 teaspoon green tea powder (matcha)
  • 1 cup water
  • 1/4 cup white sugar
  • 1/2 cup cornstarch (for rolling out the dough)

Directions:

  1. Step

    Wrap red bean paste in aluminum foil and place in the freezer until solid, at least 1 hour.

  2. Step

    Mix glutinous rice flour and green tea powder thoroughly in a microwave-safe bowl. Stir in water, then sugar; mix until smooth.

  3. Step

    Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and microwave for 3 minutes 30 seconds.

  4. Step

    Meanwhile, remove red bean paste from the freezer and divide into 8 equal balls. Set aside.

  5. Step

    Remove rice flour mixture from the microwave. Stir and heat, covered, for another 15 to 30 seconds.

  6. Step

    sDust a work surface with cornstarch. Roll about 2 tablespoons of hot rice flour mixture into a ball. Flatten the ball and place one ball of frozen red bean paste in the center. Pinch and press the dough around the bean paste until completely covered. Sprinkle with additional cornstarch and place mochi, seam-side down, in a paper muffin liner to prevent sticking.

  7. Step

    Repeat 6.Step to make remaining mochi.
